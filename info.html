JiTpro FAQ's

Just-in-Time procurement isn’t about ordering materials as soon as possible — it’s about managing the flow of procurement with intention. It keeps the project team focused on what truly matters today, while everything else waits its scheduled turn. When procurement is planned with forethought and sequence, decisions happen at the right time, information stays clear, and materials arrive on site exactly when they’re needed. The result: fewer mistakes, less rework, and a smoother project from start to finish.

JiTpro helps General Contractors take swift control of:

  • Project scope identification
    Clarifying every trade, material, and responsibility so there are no surprises from buyout to materials, products, and services arriving on site exactly when needed.
  • Capturing all pending design items — with responsibilities and delivery dates
    We expose missing information early and assign clear accountability, so decisions don’t drift into the critical path.
  • Calling out and assigning deadlines for all information, focusing on missing or undeveloped specs/selections
    No more guessing. No more waiting. Designers and owners know exactly what they owe and when.
  • Buyout
    Structured buyout that aligns with real installation needs, long-lead risks, and subcontractor sequencing.
  • Submittal/Selection gathering
    Collecting what’s needed and submitting in a clear order, instead of overwhelming the design team with everything at once.
  • Submittal reviews — including multi-disciplinary coordination
    We help ensure each submittal is reviewed for clashes, trade impacts, and adjacent work before it’s released. JiTpro gives your teams the right time needed to conduct multi-disciplinary reviews.
  • Approvals
    Driving approvals through a predictable sequence so fabrication and purchasing can begin on time.
  • Fabrication/Purchasing of all products, materials, and services
    Everything moves forward with clear lead times, accountability, and early visibility into risk.
  • Delivery of all products, materials, and services — correctly and just-in-time
    Materials show up when the field needs them — not months early, not weeks late. This reduces damage, rework, and storage issues while protecting the schedule.

The Bottom Line

JiTpro gives smaller GCs the same disciplined procurement control that large contractors rely on — without adding staff, stress, or complexity. It lets them build with confidence, protect their profit, and keep their promises.

Even with a strong procurement system in place, there will always be delays that sit completely outside the contractor’s control. You can’t control the client’s decision-making pace, and you can’t control how fast the design team responds.

But you are responsible for communicating what’s required — clearly, early, and repeatedly — so everyone understands the timelines needed to meet the project completion date.

When those timelines slip because clients or consultants don’t deliver information on time, the contractor needs to be able to show exactly why the project is slipping and document the added cost of that delay.

That’s where disciplined procurement management matters most. It creates a visible sequence of required decisions, submittals, approvals, and design deliverables. So when something falls behind, you can point to it, track it, and protect your schedule — and your profit — with facts instead of friction.

This is exactly what JiTpro is here for.

Often, contractors start projects already behind the 8-ball. Structural steel packages, doors & windows, cabinets, and many other long-lead items may already be late, the day the contractor starts the project. Who is responsible for the late procurement of these items? JiTpro gives the contractor the ability to quickly identify items that were late prior to the start of the project and provide realistic feedback to the client. When this is done correctly, the project team can focus on working together to expedite what they can and then provide realistic expectations to the client regarding schedule impacts that cannot be avoided.

Too many projects fall into the same trap: a general contractor takes on the job but waits too long to take control of procurement. Submittals start to drift. Missing design information stacks up. Long-lead items become a moving target with no clear plan to push them through the process. By the time the GC raises concerns, the warning sounds reactive instead of proactive — not because they’re wrong, but because they can’t show the pathway they should have established on day one.

The Reactionary GC

This contractor tells the client and architect that delays are coming, but without a structured procurement schedule or clear documentation, the warning feels like guesswork.

Even when the GC sees the train wreck forming, the warning gets dismissed as fear-driven intuition or excuse-making.

There’s no breakdown of missing design decisions, no record of overdue submittals, no evidence of long-lead impacts — only the GC’s word.

The result?

  • Their credibility takes a hit.
  • Their warnings are ignored.
  • And when delays finally land, the GC carries the blame for issues that were never in their control.

The GC Who Takes Immediate Control

Now compare that to the GC who locks in procurement from day one. This contractor maps every scope item, every submittal, every pending design decision, every review cycle, and every long-lead risk into a sequenced procurement plan.

When something starts to slip — whether from the client, design team, or a subcontractor — they don’t rely on instinct. They rely on evidence:

  • Documented design deliverables with due dates
  • Logged submittal requests and response times
  • Multi-disciplinary review notes
  • Real fabrication and lead-time data
  • Clear cause-and-effect impacts on the construction schedule

When they say, “This delay impacts the project,” they can show exactly where, how, and why.

The conversation shifts from opinion to fact — from defense to professional accountability. This GC earns trust, protects their schedule, and positions themself as the calm, proactive leader the project needs.
