Founder’s Statement — JiTpro

I’ve spent my entire life in construction.

I’ve seen too many projects where teams worked hard, planned to the best of their abilities, and still watched the schedule unravel — not because of poor craftsmanship or lack of effort, but because materials, products, and services didn’t arrive when they were supposed to, were incorrect when they did, or, too often, both.

After decades managing large luxury residential and commercial projects, one pattern became impossible to ignore:

Nearly every major delay or cost overrun within the contractor’s control could be traced back to a single root cause — a procurement item that arrived late, arrived wrong, or both.

It wasn’t field execution that killed schedules; it was front-end blindness.

The buyout took longer than anticipated.
Shop drawings and submittals were rushed.
There was no time for multi-disciplinary reviews to catch clashes with adjacent work.
Submittals and selections got stuck in endless rounds of revisions.
Design teams were buried under “expedited review” requests.

By the time anyone recognized the issue, construction was already underway and it was too late to alter course. Materials and products arriving late unleashed a tidal wave of schedule impacts — throwing off sequencing, labor, and every trade that followed.

I’ve watched this story play out hundreds of times — across every project type, scale, and market.

And I realized something simple but profound:

If general contractors truly controlled procurement early in the project lifecycle, most of the schedule pain, cost overruns, and credibility damage we see in this industry would disappear.

That’s why I founded JiTpro — not to add another layer of management, but to give contractors the tools, structure, and visibility to take control of procurement from day one.

Because when you control procurement, you control your schedule.
And when you control your schedule, you protect your profit — and your reputation.

JiTpro gives you the tools to do exactly that.
